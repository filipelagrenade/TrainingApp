# Comprehensive Exercise Library

This document contains an extensive library of exercises. Each exercise includes name, description, step-by-step instructions, primary and secondary muscles targeted, equipment type, and exercise classification.

## CHEST EXERCISES

### BARBELL CHEST EXERCISES

**Barbell Bench Press**
- Description: The foundational chest exercise performed lying on a flat bench, pressing a barbell from chest level to full arm extension.
- Instructions: Lie flat on a bench with feet firmly planted on the floor. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width. Unrack the bar and position it over your chest with arms extended. Lower the bar in a controlled manner to mid-chest level, keeping elbows at roughly 45-75 degrees from your torso. Press the bar back up to the starting position by extending your arms fully. Keep your shoulder blades retracted and maintain a slight arch in your lower back throughout the movement.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Incline Barbell Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press performed on an inclined bench to emphasize the upper portion of the chest.
- Instructions: Set the bench to a 30-45 degree incline. Lie back with feet flat on the floor. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width and unrack it. Lower the bar to your upper chest, just below the collarbone. Press the bar back up to full arm extension. Keep your shoulder blades squeezed together and maintain contact between your upper back and the bench throughout.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Decline Barbell Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press performed on a declined bench to emphasize the lower chest fibers.
- Instructions: Set the bench to a 15-30 degree decline and secure your legs under the pads. Lie back and grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width. Unrack the bar and position it over your lower chest. Lower the bar to your lower chest in a controlled manner. Press the bar back up to full extension. Maintain control throughout as the decline position can make the bar path less stable.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Close-Grip Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press with a narrower grip that shifts emphasis to the triceps while still working the chest.
- Instructions: Lie on a flat bench with feet planted. Grip the bar with hands shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower. Unrack and position the bar over your chest. Lower the bar to your lower chest, keeping elbows tucked close to your sides at about 30 degrees. Press the bar back up to full extension.
- Primary Muscles: Chest, Triceps
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Floor Press**
- Description: A bench press variation performed lying on the floor, which limits range of motion and eliminates leg drive.
- Instructions: Lie on the floor with knees bent and feet flat. Position yourself under a barbell in a rack or have a partner hand it to you. Grip the bar at bench press width. Lower the bar until your upper arms touch the floor, pause briefly, then press back up. Keep your shoulder blades retracted and core braced throughout.
- Primary Muscles: Chest, Triceps
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Paused Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press with a deliberate pause at the bottom to eliminate momentum and increase time under tension.
- Instructions: Perform a standard bench press but pause the bar on your chest for 1-3 seconds before pressing. Keep tension in your muscles during the pause without relaxing. Press explosively after the pause.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

### DUMBBELL CHEST EXERCISES

**Dumbbell Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press performed with dumbbells, allowing for greater range of motion and independent arm movement.
- Instructions: Lie on a flat bench holding a dumbbell in each hand at chest level. Press the dumbbells up until your arms are extended, bringing them together slightly at the top. Lower the dumbbells with control, allowing a deeper stretch than a barbell allows. Keep your feet planted and shoulder blades retracted throughout.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Incline Dumbbell Press**
- Description: Dumbbell press on an incline bench emphasizing the upper chest.
- Instructions: Set the bench to 30-45 degrees. Sit back with a dumbbell in each hand resting on your thighs. Kick them up to shoulder level as you lie back. Press the dumbbells up and slightly together. Lower with control to get a deep stretch in the upper chest. Keep elbows at about 45 degrees from your torso.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Decline Dumbbell Press**
- Description: Dumbbell press on a decline bench emphasizing the lower chest.
- Instructions: Secure your legs on a decline bench and lie back holding dumbbells. Press them up over your lower chest until arms are extended. Lower the dumbbells to the sides of your lower chest with control.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Fly**
- Description: An isolation exercise for the chest involving a wide arcing motion with arms nearly straight.
- Instructions: Lie on a flat bench holding dumbbells above your chest with palms facing each other. With a slight bend in your elbows, lower the dumbbells out to your sides in a wide arc until you feel a stretch in your chest. Squeeze your chest muscles to bring the dumbbells back together above your chest. Maintain the same elbow bend throughout.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Incline Dumbbell Fly**
- Description: Fly movement on an incline bench targeting the upper chest.
- Instructions: Set bench to 30-45 degrees. Lie back with dumbbells extended above your upper chest, palms facing each other. Lower the dumbbells in an arc to your sides, maintaining a slight elbow bend. Feel the stretch in your upper chest, then squeeze to bring the dumbbells back together.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Pullover**
- Description: A movement that stretches the chest and lats by lowering weight behind the head while lying on a bench.
- Instructions: Lie across a bench with your upper back supported and hips dropped below bench level. Hold one dumbbell with both hands above your chest, arms slightly bent. Lower the dumbbell in an arc behind your head until you feel a deep stretch in your chest and lats. Pull the dumbbell back to the starting position using your chest and lats.
- Primary Muscles: Chest, Lats
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Core
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Squeeze Press**
- Description: A pressing movement where dumbbells are pressed together throughout to maximize chest contraction.
- Instructions: Lie on a flat bench with dumbbells together above your chest, palms facing each other. Press the dumbbells together forcefully and maintain this pressure throughout. Lower and press the dumbbells while keeping them squeezed together.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

### CABLE CHEST EXERCISES

**Cable Crossover**
- Description: A cable fly movement performed standing, crossing the handles in front of the body.
- Instructions: Set both cable pulleys to the highest position. Stand in the center and grab a handle in each hand. Step forward into a split stance with a slight forward lean. With arms slightly bent, bring the handles down and together in front of your lower chest, crossing them slightly. Control the return to feel a stretch in your chest.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Cable
- Type: Strength

**Low Cable Crossover**
- Description: Cable crossover with pulleys set low, emphasizing the upper chest.
- Instructions: Set both cable pulleys to the lowest position. Grab the handles and step forward into a split stance. With slightly bent arms, bring the handles up and together in front of your upper chest or face level. Squeeze your upper chest at the top, then control the return.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Cable
- Type: Strength

**Cable Fly**
- Description: A fly movement using cables, which provides constant tension throughout the range of motion.
- Instructions: Set a bench between cable stations with pulleys at floor level. Lie on the bench and grab the handles. With a slight bend in your elbows, bring the handles up and together above your chest in an arcing motion. Slowly lower back down to feel a stretch.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Cable
- Type: Strength

**Cable Chest Press**
- Description: A pressing movement using cables, providing constant tension through the entire range.
- Instructions: Set cables at chest height. Stand facing away from the machine in a split stance. Hold a handle in each hand at chest level. Press forward until arms are extended, bringing hands together slightly. Control the return.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Cable
- Type: Strength

### MACHINE CHEST EXERCISES

**Machine Chest Press**
- Description: A pressing movement on a chest press machine providing a fixed movement path.
- Instructions: Adjust the seat so handles are at chest level. Sit with back flat against the pad. Grip the handles and press forward until arms are extended. Control the return until you feel a stretch in your chest.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Machine
- Type: Strength

**Pec Deck / Machine Fly**
- Description: An isolation machine that mimics the fly movement for direct chest work.
- Instructions: Adjust the seat height so your upper arms are parallel to the floor when gripping the handles. Sit with back against the pad. Place forearms on the pads or grip the handles. Squeeze your chest to bring the pads or handles together in front of you. Hold the contraction briefly, then control the return.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Machine
- Type: Strength

**Smith Machine Bench Press**
- Description: Bench press performed on a Smith machine with a guided bar path.
- Instructions: Position a flat bench under the Smith machine bar. Lie down and grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width. Unrack by rotating the bar and lower it to your chest. Press back up to full extension.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Machine
- Type: Strength

**Hammer Strength Chest Press**
- Description: A plate-loaded chest press machine with independent arms for balanced development.
- Instructions: Adjust the seat so handles are at chest level. Load the weight and sit with back against the pad. Press the handles forward until arms are extended. Control the return to starting position.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Machine
- Type: Strength

### BODYWEIGHT CHEST EXERCISES

**Push-Up**
- Description: The fundamental bodyweight pushing exercise performed in a plank position.
- Instructions: Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor, keeping elbows at 45 degrees from your body. Push back up to the starting position. Keep your core tight and body straight throughout.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Wide Push-Up**
- Description: Push-up with hands placed wider than standard to increase chest stretch and activation.
- Instructions: Set up like a standard push-up but place hands significantly wider than shoulder-width. Lower your body, allowing elbows to flare more than normal. Push back up.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Diamond Push-Up**
- Description: Push-up with hands close together forming a diamond shape, emphasizing triceps.
- Instructions: Start in a push-up position with hands close together, thumbs and index fingers forming a diamond shape. Lower your body while keeping elbows close to your sides. Push back up.
- Primary Muscles: Chest, Triceps
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Incline Push-Up**
- Description: Push-up with hands elevated on a surface, making it easier and emphasizing lower chest.
- Instructions: Place hands on an elevated surface like a bench, step, or wall. Walk feet back until body is straight at an angle. Perform a push-up, lowering chest toward the surface.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Decline Push-Up**
- Description: Push-up with feet elevated, increasing difficulty and emphasizing upper chest.
- Instructions: Place feet on an elevated surface and hands on the floor. Your body should angle downward toward your head. Perform push-ups in this position.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Clapping Push-Up**
- Description: An explosive push-up where you push off the ground hard enough to clap before landing.
- Instructions: Start in a push-up position. Lower your body, then explosively push up so your hands leave the ground. Quickly clap your hands together and return them to the floor to catch yourself.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

**Dip (Chest Emphasis)**
- Description: A compound pushing exercise performed on parallel bars with a forward lean to target chest.
- Instructions: Grip parallel bars and support yourself with arms straight. Lean your torso forward about 30 degrees. Lower yourself by bending elbows until you feel a stretch in your chest, keeping the forward lean. Push back up to straight arms.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Bodyweight
- Type: Strength

- Primary Muscles: Chest

- Equipment: Kettlebell
- Type: Strength

### BAND CHEST EXERCISES

**Banded Push-Up**
- Description: Push-up with a resistance band across the back for added resistance at the top.
- Instructions: Loop a resistance band across your upper back and hold the ends in your hands. Get into a push-up position with the band providing resistance. Perform push-ups; the band adds difficulty especially at the top.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt, Core
- Equipment: Band
- Type: Strength

**Band Chest Press**
- Description: Pressing movement against band resistance while standing.
- Instructions: Anchor a band at chest height behind you. Hold the band handles or ends at chest level. Step forward to create tension. Press forward until arms are extended. Return with control.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Band
- Type: Strength

**Band Fly**
- Description: Fly movement using band resistance for constant tension.
- Instructions: Anchor a band behind you at chest height. Hold the ends with arms extended to the sides. With a slight elbow bend, bring your hands together in front of your chest, squeezing at the top. Control the return.
- Primary Muscles: Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Front Delt
- Equipment: Band
- Type: Strength

## BACK EXERCISES

### BARBELL BACK EXERCISES

**Barbell Deadlift**
- Description: The king of all lifts, a full-body compound movement that primarily targets the posterior chain.
- Instructions: Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over mid-foot. Hinge at hips, grip bar just outside legs. Keep chest up, back flat. Drive through heels, extending hips and knees simultaneously. Lock out at the top with shoulders back. Lower by hinging at hips first, then bending knees once bar passes them.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Hamstrings
- Secondary Muscles: Glutes, Quads, Core, Traps, Forearms
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Barbell Row (Bent Over Row)**
- Description: A fundamental pulling movement targeting the entire back musculature.
- Instructions: Hinge forward at hips until torso is roughly 45 degrees. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder width. Pull bar to lower chest/upper abdomen, squeezing shoulder blades together. Lower under control.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Lats
- Secondary Muscles: Biceps, Rear Delt, Rhomboids
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Pendlay Row**
- Description: A strict rowing variation where the bar starts from the floor each rep, eliminating momentum.
- Instructions: Set up like a deadlift with chest parallel to floor. Pull bar explosively to lower chest, then lower it completely to the floor. Reset and repeat.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Lats
- Secondary Muscles: Biceps, Rear Delt, Core
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**T-Bar Row**
- Description: A rowing movement using a T-bar setup that allows heavy loading with a neutral grip.
- Instructions: Straddle the bar with feet wide. Grip the handles or bar with a close grip. Row the weight to your chest, squeezing your back at the top. Lower with control.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Lats
- Secondary Muscles: Biceps, Rear Delt, Rhomboids
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Barbell Shrug**
- Description: An isolation exercise for the trapezius muscles.
- Instructions: Hold a barbell with arms extended at your sides. Elevate your shoulders straight up toward your ears, hold briefly, then lower.
- Primary Muscles: Traps
- Secondary Muscles: Forearms
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Rack Pull**
- Description: A partial deadlift performed from elevated pins, focusing on the lockout portion.
- Instructions: Set safety pins at knee height or just below. Grip the bar and perform the top portion of a deadlift, focusing on hip extension and back engagement.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Traps
- Secondary Muscles: Glutes, Hamstrings, Forearms
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

### DUMBBELL BACK EXERCISES

**Dumbbell Row (Single Arm)**
- Description: A unilateral rowing movement allowing full range of motion and balanced development.
- Instructions: Place one knee and hand on a bench for support. Row the dumbbell to your hip, keeping elbow close to body. Squeeze at top, lower with control.
- Primary Muscles: Lats, Back
- Secondary Muscles: Biceps, Rear Delt, Core
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift**
- Description: A hip hinge movement emphasizing the hamstrings and glutes with dumbbells.
- Instructions: Hold dumbbells at thighs. Push hips back while maintaining slight knee bend and flat back. Lower until you feel a hamstring stretch, then drive hips forward to return.
- Primary Muscles: Hamstrings, Glutes
- Secondary Muscles: Lower Back, Core
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Shrug**
- Description: Trap isolation using dumbbells for a more natural arm position.
- Instructions: Hold dumbbells at sides with palms facing body. Elevate shoulders toward ears, pause at top, lower slowly.
- Primary Muscles: Traps
- Secondary Muscles: Forearms
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Dumbbell Pullover**
- Description: A movement targeting lats and chest through a wide arc of motion.
- Instructions: Lie perpendicular across a bench, only upper back supported. Hold dumbbell overhead with both hands. Lower it behind your head in an arc, feeling the stretch, then pull it back over chest.
- Primary Muscles: Lats, Chest
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Serratus
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

**Renegade Row**
- Description: A combination of plank and row that challenges the core while working the back.
- Instructions: Get in push-up position holding dumbbells. Alternating arms, row one dumbbell to hip while stabilizing with the other. Minimize hip rotation.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Lats
- Secondary Muscles: Core, Biceps, Shoulders
- Equipment: Dumbbell
- Type: Strength

## SHOULDER EXERCISES

### BARBELL SHOULDER EXERCISES

**Overhead Press (Military Press)**
- Description: The foundational shoulder pressing movement performed standing.
- Instructions: Hold bar at shoulder level with grip slightly wider than shoulders. Press bar overhead until arms are locked. Lower under control.
- Primary Muscles: Front Delt
- Secondary Muscles: Side Delt, Triceps, Core
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Push Press**
- Description: An overhead press with leg drive allowing heavier weights.
- Instructions: Dip slightly at knees, then explosively drive bar overhead using leg momentum. Lock out overhead, lower to shoulders.
- Primary Muscles: Front Delt
- Secondary Muscles: Side Delt, Triceps, Legs, Core
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Behind the Neck Press**
- Description: Overhead press lowering bar behind the head (requires good shoulder mobility).
- Instructions: Lower bar behind head to upper back level, then press back up. Use lighter weight and full control.
- Primary Muscles: Front Delt, Side Delt
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Traps
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Barbell Upright Row**
- Description: A pulling movement that targets the traps and side delts.
- Instructions: Hold bar with narrow grip at thighs. Pull bar up along body to chin level, leading with elbows. Lower with control.
- Primary Muscles: Traps, Side Delt
- Secondary Muscles: Biceps, Front Delt
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Bradford Press**
- Description: A continuous pressing movement alternating in front and behind the head.
- Instructions: Press bar from front to just over head, lower behind neck, press back over, lower to front. No lockout at top.
- Primary Muscles: Front Delt, Side Delt
- Secondary Muscles: Triceps
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

- Secondary Muscles: None
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

- Secondary Muscles: Traps, Shoulders
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Power Snatch**
- Description: A snatch caught in partial squat rather than full overhead squat.
- Instructions: Pull bar explosively, catch overhead in quarter squat, stand.
- Primary Muscles: Back, Shoulders
- Secondary Muscles: Legs, Traps
- Equipment: Barbell
- Type: Strength

**Hang Clean**

**Hang Snatch**
